Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging (CLSA)

https://www.clsa-elcv.ca/

One of the projects that would be supported by the workshop that was summarized in Doiron, Raina, and Fortier (2013). Also mentioned in Doiron et al. (2017).

Began as a Maelstrom partner in 2017. https://maelstrom-research.org/study/clsa https://doi.org/10.1017/s0714980809990055 https://doi.org/10.1093/ije/dyz173

Seems less concerned with data-drivenness, more focused on the areas that the work will address, as well as participant and community engagement.

Canadian Partnership for Tomorrow (CPTP)

Renamed the Canadian Partnership for Tomorrow’s Health (CanPath); https://canpath.ca/ Canada’s largest population health study.

One of the projects that would be supported by the workshop that was summarized in Doiron, Raina, and Fortier (2013). Also mentioned in Doiron et al. (2017).

Current Maelstrom partner, began in 2014. https://maelstrom-research.org/network/cptp-1 https://doi.org/10.1503/cmaj.170292

Leadership seems to have many members from CARTaGENE.

CARTaGENE

https://cartagene.qc.ca/en/ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CARTaGENE_biobank CARTaGENE is a long-term cohort study for the investigation of modifiable environmental and lifestyle factors and the genomic determinants of chronic diseases in Quebec, Canada.

Initially founded by Dr. Claude Laberge and Prof. Bartha Maria Knoppers, CARTaGENE is today under the scientific direction of Dr. Guillaume Lettre, Dr. Simon Gravel and Dr. Vikki Ho.

a regional cohort member of the Canadian Partnership for Tomorrow’s Health (CanPath)

Current Maelstrom partner, began in 2018. https://maelstrom-research.org/study/cag https://doi.org/10.1093/ije/dys160

euCanSHare

An EU-Canada Joint Infrastructure for Next-Generation Multi-Study Heart Research

euCanSHare is an Europe-Canada joint infrastructure and network for enhancing multi-cohort studies in cardiovascular research. The mission of euCanSHare is to develop the first integrated data platform for cross-border data sharing and personalized medicine research in cardiology, integrating cohorts from Europe, Canada and beyond. The key objectives are the following:

To build a centralized platform for cardiovascular data across Europe, Canada and beyond
To facilitate big data research in personalized cardiovascular medicine
To apply new legal framework for Open Science across continents
To establish the largest international research community in cardiology

Studies described on the Maelstrom catalogue may differ from the ones on the EuCanShare website.

Started partership with Maelstrom in 2018 https://maelstrom-research.org/network/eucanshare http://www.eucanshare.eu/

CAPACIty

CAnadian PediAtric diabetes ConsortIum (CAPACIty) is a network of 15 childhood diabetes centers from across Canada. We are partnering with patients/families and health care professionals to jointly design and develop a Canadawide childhood diabetes registry and research platform. The registry will enable us to improve diabetes care and health outcomes for Canadian youth through comparison of diabetes care quality and outcomes between Canadian diabetes centers, quality improvement initiatives, patient-informed research initiatives across Canada, and successful advocacy work.
