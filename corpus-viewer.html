This all relates to the idea that data workers, who are explicitly data workers in identity, tooling, background, community of practice, etc, and especially those who work with an eye toward developing _research infrastructure_, have a more finely fined curatorial lens, since they must constantly think about reconciling differences between inputs and outcomes.
This is also true on a "grander scale" with Maelstrom, who work on a more abstract level, and who cope with these challenges through adherence to procedure.
And I'm sure that from the perspective of the study originators, CITF is very procedural too, perhaps even to a fault, in their minds.
Which again brings me back to the notion that data management is management, and how data sharing infrastructures mask low-scale collaborative experiences using technical and managerial processes and logics.
