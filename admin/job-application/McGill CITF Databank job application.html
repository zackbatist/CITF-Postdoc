    The CITF Databank has an open position for a full-time Research Assistant with a focus on stakeholder research. The successful candidate will be instrumental in promoting and expanding the use of COVID-19 research data. We seek an individual with a background in meta-research and epidemiology, or a related discipline, who has demonstrated skills in science community outreach and communication. Creativity, organization, and a collaborative mindset are attributes we value highly.

    The CITF Databank centralizes and standardizes data from over 50 Canadian COVID-19 studies, providing researchers with a range of standardized pandemic data at no cost. This databank was established to augment the impact of the studies funded by the COVID-19 Immunity Task Force (CITF).

    Launched by the Government of Canada in April 2020, the CITF’s mandate was to catalyze, support, fund, and harmonize knowledge on SARS-CoV-2 immunity, providing critical pandemic management insights for federal, provincial, and territorial decision-makers. The CITF operated until March 31, 2024. The CITF Databank continues the work of the CITF Data Management and Analysis unit, in partnership with a Science Advisory Board and a Data Access Committee.  Our Governance Framework is aligned with open science and FAIR data principles, and we have a mandate to promote and understand the use of the data for research.

    Position Summary:

    • Identify Canadian and international stakeholders (public, governmental, private researchers, graduate education departments)
    • Help develop a Stakeholder Research plan
    • Help develop and evaluate information materials and workshops promoting and facilitating data access
    • Conduct focus group research and incorporate user feedback
    • Report progress and research results to Science Advisory Board and in peer reviewed publications
    • Work with small team of epidemiologists and data scientists to document, understand, and enhance the curated research data
    • Maintain data security and participant confidentiality.

    Qualifications:

    • Required
      • Masters degree or higher in a relevant field such as population science related field (epidemiology, public health, applied economics, sociology), Information Science, Philosophy of Science, Communications
      • Excellent written and oral communication skills
      • Strong interpersonal and problem-solving skills. Ability to coordinate across teams.
    • Assets
      • Complementary experience (3+ years): If education was mainly quantitative and scientific, practical experience in qualitative research, focus groups, or knowledge translation; otherwise, practical experience in population sciences or medicine
      • Knowledge of meta-research (research about research), open science, and FAIR data principles and organizations
      • Understanding of measurement and data quality in human observational research.
      • Bilingual
      • Agile project management skills

    Work Experience 1 Job Title: Founder and Maintainer Company: open-archaeo

    Role Description: I undertake research on academic open source software engineering practices using open-archaeo, a comprehensive list of tools and resources developed by and for archaeologists that I have been maintaining as an independent side-project since 2018 (Batist & Roe 2023, https://doi.org/10.5334/joad.111). I leveraged this dataset, supplemented with data pulled from the GitHub API, to perform statistical and network analyses of the collaborative experiences afforded by the open source model (Batist & Roe 2024, https://doi.org/10.11141/ia.67.13). I was able to trace how archaeologists use various languages, forges, licenses and supporting features (e.g. issues, stars, pull requests), and to discern trends regarding projects’ longevity, degree of community participation, and overall structure of collaborative ties. I am currently expanding this work to examine additional aspects of open collaborative experiences, including citation analysis of data papers (with an emphasis on identifying patterns of self-citation), and adherence to opinionated coding paradigms.

    Work Experience 2 Job Title: Research Assistant Company: University of Toronto (E-CURATORS)

    Role Description: I planned and implemented a federally-funded research project about the application of digital methods in archaeological research. I was in charge of collecting and analyzing data, coordinating teams of international collaborators, training and managing research assistants, setting up and maintaining the project’s tech stack and data management workflows, and publishing our primary set of findings (Batist et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.1515/opar-2020-0217). One significant aspect of my work involved designing and maintaining graph databases based on Neo4j, which served as the cornerstone for our rigorous qualitative and statistical analyses of digital archaeology research practices. I was also responsible for leading cross-institutional research initiatives, training other project participants on the use of graph databases and other critical methods and practices, and ensuring that strong data quality standards were being met.

    Work Experience 3 Job Title: Database Manager Company: McMaster University (Stelida Naxos Archaeology Project)

    Role Description: I was responsible for designing and maintaining relational databases in fieldwork and analytical contexts. This involved developing workflows and interfaces for inputting and accessing data, coordinating with a wide array of interdisciplinary collaborators and specialists, training students and cross-appointed personnel, and maintaining various related aspects of the project’s tech stack. I led the development of QuARI, a database interface designed to facilitate the irregular, iterative and non-linear workflows that are commonly employed in analysis of prehistoric stone tools (Contreras et al. 2021, https://doi.org/10.1017/aap.2021.11). These experiences directly inspired my doctoral research concerning the social and technical infrastructures that scaffold archaeological data work, especially in contexts of reuse.

    Work Experience 4 Job Title: Research Assistant Company: Molecular Pharmacology Laboratory, Lady Davis Institute for Medical Research

    Role Description: I prepared cell samples and reagents, and assisted with biomolecular analysis and imaging.
